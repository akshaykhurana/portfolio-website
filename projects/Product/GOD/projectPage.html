G.O.D.

2016
Classroom project at NID, Bangalore
As a group with Milad Thaha, Manikanta Pollisetti, Devang Kantharia guided by Michael Longford (York University)

An exercise in Speculative Design aimed at teaching skills in rapid prototyping and out-of-the-box thinking. This module titled "FutureSense" was part of an interdisciplinary elective between the three NID campuses.

poster

process

Brief

The brief for this module was to think way out-of-the-box and come up with a product that could manifest itself in a far-flung future.
In part, this is inspired by the work of Dunne and Raby especially their work titled "Designs for an Overpopulated Planet: Foragers, 2009". The dystopian worldview illustrated by Dunne and Raby coupled with recent developments in AI were fundamental to creating the backstory for this exploration.

The point of this exercise is not to create believable technology but to induce a sense of discomfort in the audience visiting the exhibit by exaggerating the technology of the future in a way that makes it uncomfortably real. This effect is achieved by creating seemingly real artefacts that are loosely based on modern technologies.

This project was exhibited at the FutureSense exhibit at Gandhinagar at the end of the program module.

Concept

The project envisions a future where humans are connected as a hive mind to the internet through implants directly embedded in their bodies. In this day and age, privacy is of utmost concern but so is the importance of being "always connected"

Here's a couple of pointers to make sense of the coming visuals:

1. The hive mind is governed by an all-powerful godlike AI aptly called G.O.D. All humans connect to each other and G.O.D. through devices that can alter their vision and auditory streams.

2. Connected users experience G.O.D through a series of visions and auditory hallucinations commonly refered to as "Visitations"

3. Additionally, everyone has a neural uplink to GOD directly implanted into their spine to intercept and relay brain activity directly to the network. The uplink is only active when approved by the human and all personal data remains safely stored in an internal organ augmenting the liver.
